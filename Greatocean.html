Great Ocean Road

The Great Ocean Road is one of Australia's most iconic coastal drives, stretching approximately 243 kilometers (151 miles) along the southeastern coast of Victoria. It offers breathtaking views of rugged cliffs, pristine beaches, lush rainforests, and unique rock formations

History Construction: Built between 1919 and 1932, the road was constructed by returning soldiers from World War I as a war memorial dedicated to their fallen comrades. It is often referred to as the world's largest war memorial. Purpose: Initially built to connect isolated communities along the coast, the road quickly became a popular tourist attraction due to its stunning scenery. Cultural Significance: The Great Ocean Road is recognized as a significant part of Australia’s heritage and was added to the Australian National Heritage List in 2011.

Visiting Information

  • Key Attractions Along the Route Torquay: Known as Australia’s surfing capital and home to the famous Bells Beach. Lorne: A charming seaside town ideal for stopping for food, beaches, and waterfalls. Apollo Bay: Offers beautiful beaches and serves as the gateway to the Great Otway National Park.
  • OPrices and Costs While driving along the Great Ocean Road is free, there are associated costs for activities and attractions: Petrol Costs: Average petrol cost: AUD 1.80–2.10 per liter (varies by location and time). Total fuel cost for a round trip from Melbourne (around 500 km) may range between AUD 60–100, depending on fuel efficiency. Tours and Packages: Accommodations:
  • Budget hostels: AUD 30–50 per night.
  • Mid-range hotels: AUD 100–200 per night.
  • Luxury stays: AUD 250+ per night.
  • Attraction Tickets Tips for Travelers Best Time to Visit: Spring (September–November) and autumn (March–May) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. Driving Conditions: The road is winding and requires careful driving. Drive on the left side in Australia. Duration: A minimum of 2–3 days is recommended to fully enjoy the route and its attractions. Pack Essentials: Include a camera, sunscreen, water, snacks, and comfortable walking shoes.

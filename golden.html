Golden Temple

  • The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is one of the holiest and most significant religious sites for Sikhs worldwide.
    1. Location: Address: Golden Temple, Harmandir Sahib, Amritsar, Punjab, India. The temple is located at the center of Amritsar, and its stunning golden architecture is surrounded by a large artificial lake known as the Amrit Sarovar.
  • History:
    • The Golden Temple was founded by Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru, in 1581. The construction of the temple was initiated by him, and the site was chosen near the holy tank of Amrit Sarovar.
    • The Golden Temple was later expanded and the Sarovar was constructed, with the temple being covered in gold in 1830 under the patronage of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, the leader of the Sikh Empire.
    • The temple is a symbol of the Sikh philosophy of equality, and it is known for its openness to people of all religions, castes, and backgrounds.
    • Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism, is placed inside the temple and is continuously read aloud.
      • The Golden Temple was attacked during Operation Blue Star in 1984 by the Indian Army, which aimed to remove Sikh militants from the temple. This event caused significant loss of life and damage to the temple, but it has since been restored.

    Visting Details

  • Entry Fee: Free for all visitors. The temple is open to everyone, regardless of religion, and there is no charge to enter. However, donations are encouraged to maintain the temple and its facilities. Timings: Open 24 hours, but the best time to visit is early morning for the prayer services or evening for the mesmerizing evening rituals and lighting.

    Things to Keep in Mind:

  • Dress Code: Visitors are required to dress modestly. Men should cover their heads with a cloth or a turban, and women should wear a scarf. Sleeveless tops and shorts are not allowed.
  • Footwear: Visitors must remove their footwear before entering the temple complex.
  • Silence and Respect: Maintain silence and respect the religious practices while inside the temple and in the surrounding area.
    • Cleanliness: It is important to keep the premises clean, and you will often find volunteers cleaning the area. Security Checks: There are routine security checks, so expect a brief wait before entering.
  • Langar: The Golden Temple offers free meals (Langar) to everyone, regardless of their background. It is a unique aspect of the Sikh faith, emphasizing equality and service.
